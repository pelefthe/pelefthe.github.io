Samaria Gorge This is a (short, pratical) description of our trip to Samaria Gorges.

We took the bus from Chania (bus) station at 7.45 am (there is one earlier) to Xyloskalo (it took around one hour and 15/30 minutes). (There is a coffee shop open at that time in the station and when you arrive it is possible to have a coffee/snacks). The entrance of the park is very close (the fee is 10 euros (but one may ask whether it is not free for them...), do keep your ticket, we are also controlled at the end of the park). Then the descent is very well maintained with an alpine atmosphere--it is a really nice forest. (It is not hard but one has to be careful and since it is covered with flat rocks, it is sensible to have good shoes). On the whole way, at regular intervals, there is water available (and toilets). Then you arrive at the old Samaria village-I think we arrived around noon-there is an old chapel to St Nicholas and again it is tourist friendly, with a lot of pink laurels. Then the path is mostly horizontal and follows a river. We had lunch by the river (we noticed fossils in the rocks). Finally we arrived at Agia Roumeli where we booked a ferry ticket (around 17/18 euros) to Sougia. Then we had a swim--it was a good idea since we were a bit hot-- (there are two cabins on beach to change). Also, on the beach, where we had a cell phone connection, we bought bus tickets from Sougia to Chania (each a little over 8 euros). This was wise because the buses leave immediately when the boat arrives. (The ferry departed around 5.30 pm--no need to hurry one has plenty of time--, we arrived 40/45 minutes later, a bit late on the schedule, anyway the buses were waiting for the boat-first there are coaches and then a bit further on, regular buses. There is no need to take a guided tour.)
